Authentic Thai Green Curry

This is my grandmother's recipe for Thai green curry, passed down through generations and refined over decades of cooking in Bangkok kitchens. The key is making the paste from scratch — it takes an extra 15 minutes but the depth of flavour is incomparable to anything from a jar.

Ingredients

Serves 4

For the curry paste

  • 6 green bird's eye chillies
  • 2 stalks lemongrass, white part only
  • 4 kaffir lime leaves
  • 1 thumb galangal, peeled and sliced

For the curry

  • 400ml full-fat coconut milk
  • 500g chicken thighs, cut into chunks
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tbsp palm sugar
  • 1 handful Thai basil leaves
  • 200g jasmine rice, to serve

Instructions

  1. 1

    Make the curry paste

    Combine chillies, lemongrass, galangal, kaffir lime leaves, garlic, shallots, shrimp paste and coriander roots in a mortar. Pound with the pestle for 10–12 minutes until you have a smooth, fragrant paste. Alternatively, use a food processor but the texture won't be quite as good.

    💡 Tip: Toast whole coriander seeds and cumin in a dry pan before grinding for extra depth.
  2. 2

    Fry the paste

    Heat a wok over medium-high heat. Add half the coconut milk and bring to a simmer. Add the curry paste and stir-fry for 3–4 minutes until deeply fragrant and the oil separates from the paste.

  3. 3

    Add protein and coconut milk

    Add the chicken pieces and toss to coat. Pour in the remaining coconut milk and 100ml of water or chicken stock. Bring to a gentle simmer and cook for 15 minutes until the chicken is cooked through.

  4. 4

    Season and finish

    Season with fish sauce and palm sugar to taste — you want a balance of salty, sweet and savoury. Add the Thai basil and kaffir lime leaves, stir through and remove from heat immediately.

    💡 Tip: Don't cook the basil — add it off the heat so it stays vibrant and fragrant.
  5. 5

    Serve

    Ladle over steamed jasmine rice. Garnish with extra Thai basil, sliced red chillies and a wedge of lime. Serve immediately.
